\section{Geographic attention diagnostics}
\label{app:attention}

The trained baseline's encoder attention can be sliced to expose, for
each future-tabular query position, its weighted attention over
history-spatial keys. The slice $A^{(\ell, h)}[\mathcal{Q}_{\text{fut}},
\mathcal{K}_{\text{hist}}]$ has shape $24 \times 1536$. Reshaping the
1536-dim spatial key axis to an $8 \times 8$ grid (matching
\texttt{WeatherCNN.forward}'s row-major \texttt{flatten(2)} layout) and
overlaying on the New England basemap with cartopy produces a
geographically-grounded heatmap.

Four orientation-correctness checks (slice direction, row-major
reshape, compass orientation, east-vs-west aggregate sanity) are
implemented in \texttt{scripts/attention\_maps.py}. The aggregate
east-vs-west check passes ($\text{east} \approx 0.0070$, $\text{west}
\approx 0.0050$).
